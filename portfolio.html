Malware Presentation

Our malware presentation was based on the Melissa Virus. The Melissa Virus was a virus that infected millions of computers in early 1999. The Melissa Virus disables a number of safeguards in Word 97 or Word 2000, and if the user has the Microsoft Outlook e-mail program, causes the virus to be resent to the first 50 people in each of the user's address books. While it does not destroy files or other resources, Melissa has the potential to disable corporate and other mail servers as the ripple of e-mail distribution becomes a much larger wave. On Friday, March 26, 1999, Melissa caused the Microsoft Corporation to shut down incoming e-mail. Intel and other companies also reported being affected.


Big Data Presentation

The Big Data topic that I decided to present was Algorithmic Trading. Algo Trading utilizes inputted or imported data in order to analyze and determine if it should buy or sell a certain stock. It is able to effectively trade this stock in a matter of nanoseconds, making it faster than any human in this sector of the market. It uses Big Data by processing past history and statistics on a type of stock and is able to determine how successful or unsuccessful it was. It may have records of how well the stock has done in past week or month. In my presentation I discussed how it works, and how it often it is able to successfully trade in one business day. I also mentioned how it related to Big Data based on the sole volume of information being processed during and after each transaction or sale. Volume is also a big factor. These programs are able to purchase potentially hundreds of stocks that pertain to a particular company, before its competators, and then resale it to them for a profit. These algorithms are getting faster and faster while still dominating a large section of the market.
